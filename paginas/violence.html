Violence

  • Physical Agression

    • It occurs when the partner attacks the woman through the use of physical force such as beating. This violence can manifest itself in various forms, such as: tapas, jerks, punches, Bites, kicks, burns, cuts and strangulation. Also considered as physical abuse is the attempt to throw objects, With the intention of hurting, shaking and holding a woman tightly.


  • Rape

    • It is committed through acts or attempts of sexual intercourse under duress or physical force. Sexually violent acts can occur in different circumstances, such as: forced sex within marriage or dating, demand for sex as payment of favors, sexual abuse of mentally or physically incapable persons, sexual violence also occurs: It is not only forcing the sex that is conspicuous Such as sexual violence. Forcing a woman to engage in sexual acts that cause discomfort or disgust, such as fetishes, is also violence, denial of the right to use contraceptives or to take other measures to protect against sexually transmitted diseases and forced abortion.


  • Humiliaton

    • It refers to actions that cause psychological and emotional damage to the woman, Such as threats, embarrassment, cursing and diminishing self-esteem, breaking women's objects, Moral devaluation or public debauchery regarding women are listed as types of emotional violence.


  • Expose Intimate Life

    • Leaking photos or intimate videos on social networks as a form of revenge. The woman exposed in a sexual or nudist scene undergoes greater social and affective rejection than The man caught in the same situation.


  • LGBT Prejudice

    • Violence against LGBT people-lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender-Are actions that can occur both at the hands of individuals or groups, And by the application of government laws aimed at the people who Contrary to the rules of heterocompromotivity. The definitions for the term refer variably to antipathy, Contempt, homophobia, aversion and irrational fear. Prejudice is observed Such as critical and hostile behavior, as well as discrimination and violence based on the perception that all types of non-heterosexual sexual orientation are negative.

  • Feminicide

    • Femicide: Femicide is a gender-based hate crime term, broadly defined as the murder of women, But the definitions vary depending on the cultural context.

    • Intentional and violent deaths of women as a result of their sex;

    • They are not isolated events in the lives of women because they are the result of differences in power between men and women in the different socioeconomic contexts in which they occur and, at the same time, a condition for maintaining these differences.

    Types:

    • Intimate femicide: those crimes committed by men with whom the victim has or had an intimate relationship, family, cohabitation or the like.

    • Non-intimate femicide: those committed by men with whom the victim did not have intimate, family or cohabitation relationships, but with whom there was a relationship of trust, hierarchy or friendship, such as friends or co-workers, Health workers, employers.

    • Femicides by connection: are those in which the women were murdered because they were in the "fire line" of a man who tried to kill another woman, ie are cases in which adult women or girls try to intervene to prevent The practice of a crime against another woman and end up dying.
